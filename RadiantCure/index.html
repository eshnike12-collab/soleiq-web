Cellular Energy Boost

Red light dissociates inhibitory nitric oxide from cytochrome c oxidase, restoring the mitochondrial electron transport chain and massively increasing ATP production — the fundamental fuel for every healing process in the body.

Accelerated Tissue Repair

PBM stimulates fibroblast proliferation and accelerates keratinocyte migration, dramatically speeding up re-epithelialization of wounds. Collagen synthesis increases, strengthening repaired tissue.

3× faster wound closure in clinical trials

Inflammation Reduction

Red light suppresses pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1β) and shifts macrophage polarization from destructive M1 to reparative M2 — resolving chronic inflammation that keeps wounds stalled.

Significant TNF-α and IL-1β reduction

Angiogenesis & Circulation

PBM upregulates VEGF and stimulates endothelial cells to form new blood vessels — restoring oxygen and nutrient delivery to ischemic tissue and promoting vasodilation.

Increased VEGF expression & microvascular density

Nerve Regeneration

Near-infrared light penetrates deeply to reach peripheral nerves, stimulating axonal regrowth, restoring sensation, and reducing neuropathic pain — especially valuable in diabetic neuropathy.

Improved sensory nerve function

Collagen & Skin Health

By activating TGF-β1 signalling, PBM drives fibroblasts to produce high-quality collagen Type I and III — improving wound tensile strength, reducing scar formation, and rejuvenating skin.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about RediantCure and red light therapy.

Photobiomodulation (PBM), also known as red light therapy or low-level laser therapy (LLLT), is the therapeutic use of low-power red (620–700 nm) and near-infrared (700–1,100 nm) light to stimulate cellular healing. Unlike UV light, PBM is non-ionizing and non-thermal — it does not damage DNA or burn tissue. Instead, it is absorbed by mitochondria, triggering a cascade of natural healing responses including increased energy production (ATP), reduced inflammation, and accelerated tissue repair.

Yes. The 2025 Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology (JAAD) international consensus statement — developed by a 21-member expert panel — confirmed that PBM is a safe treatment modality for adult patients and explicitly confirmed it does not induce DNA damage. Decades of clinical trials have documented minimal adverse effects. Standard precautions include avoiding direct eye exposure and not applying PBM over active malignancies.

Clinical results vary by condition and severity, but most wound healing trials report significant improvements within 4–8 weeks of regular use. For diabetic foot ulcers, Borges et al. (2024) reported measurable improvements in wound healing index by day 30, with continued improvement through 12 weeks of daily treatment. Anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects are often noticed more quickly — sometimes within the first few sessions.

RediantCure is designed primarily for chronic wound care — including diabetic foot ulcers, venous leg ulcers, and post-surgical wounds. The underlying PBM technology has also been clinically studied for: muscle and joint pain, inflammatory skin conditions, nerve regeneration in peripheral neuropathy, post-operative healing acceleration, and general skin rejuvenation.

Most clinical protocols use PBM 3–5 times per week for wound healing, with each session lasting 2–10 minutes per treatment site. Daily use — as enabled by wearable platforms like our SoleIQ insole concept — has shown excellent results in home-based trials. Regular, consistently dosed sessions produce cumulative healing benefits that infrequent sessions cannot replicate.

Absolutely. Home-based PBM delivery is a core part of our mission. Randomised controlled trials — including Haze et al. (2022) and Borges et al. (2024) — have demonstrated that patient-self-administered home PBM achieves outcomes comparable to clinic-based treatment. Our wearable insole platform enables passive daily light therapy during normal footwear use with no positioning required.

Our devices deliver dual-wavelength therapy: red light at 630–660 nm and near-infrared at 810–850 nm. Red light targets superficial wound bed cells, keratinocytes, and inflammatory pathways at 1–5 mm depth. NIR light penetrates 5–10 mm, reaching fibroblasts, endothelial cells, and peripheral nerves. Clinical trials consistently show combined red + NIR protocols outperform single-wavelength approaches.

Coverage varies by country, insurer, and clinical indication. PBM is increasingly recognised by wound care guidelines and some payers cover it as part of chronic wound management. Our clinical team can provide documentation supporting insurance pre-authorisation. We also offer flexible payment options and work with healthcare providers to integrate RediantCure into existing reimbursable care pathways.
